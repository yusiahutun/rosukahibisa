List of female ABC news anchors you should watch in 2022

Publish date: 2024-07-16

The issue of gender inequality has been the order of the day at the workplace for many decades, but the narrative has been changing slowly. This is thanks to women who were not afraid to be pioneers in male-dominated industries. Journalism is one of those fields in which women have excelled and matched their male counterparts as equal peers. The long list of female ABC news anchors is proof that women have what it takes to make it to the top.

ABC News is the division of American Broadcasting Company that deals with the news. It is owned by Disney Media Networks, a division of the Walt Disney Company. It has flagship shows such as ABC's World News Tonight, Good Morning America, Nightline, Primetime and Sunday Mornings This Week. The female ABC reporters and news anchors are the faces of news around the USA.

Female ABC news anchors

The female ABC news live anchors are the best at what they do, which is why they are at the centre of admiration worldwide. Many young girls have been inspired to dream big and chase their dreams like the female anchors at ABC. These anchors include:

1. Sandra Bookman

Sandra’s blossoming career took off in 1998 as a career for Eyewitness News and did not take long before she was promoted to weekend anchor. Before that, she had worked for WSB-TV in Atlanta, where she was a weekend anchor for almost a decade. Her experience also includes stints at WRAL-TV in Raleigh and KFDM-TV in Beaumont.

Having been an Olympic Reporter for seven years, Sandra was the sole local television reporter covering the games since Atlanta was awarded the bid until the Olympic bombing in 1996. Apart from covering the 1992 games in Barcelona, she also reported a series of stories from South Africa after the release of Nelson Mandela from jail.

2. Sade Baderinwa

Sade is the anchor for WABC-popular TV’s Eyewitness News at 5:00 and 11:00 a.m. she joined the Eyewitness team in 2003 both as a reporter and anchor of Eyewitness News at Noon. She then went to Channel 7 from WBAL-TV in Baltimore, where she led the morning and noon newscasts and produced a weekly community affairs show.

Formerly she worked for WSLS in Roanoke as a reporter, at WUSA-TV as a reporter trainee, and ABC News as a production assistant. Outside of her work, she is a stronger believer in charity and has partnered with several organizations and community leaders. As a result of her excellent work, she received the NJ Governor's Representative Award for Traffic Safety.

3. Brittany Bell

Brittany Bell is an award-winning meteorologist who boasts accolades like the American Meteorological Society's Certified Broadcast Meteorologist seal. She graduated with a Bachelor of Science in Professional Meteorology from the Mississippi State University. While still a student, she was selected to participate in Mississippi State's Great Plains storm chase, whereby she spent weeks following tornadoes in tornado alley.

She developed an interest in weather from a very young age and especially remembers the Nashville F3 tornado of 1998 as something that piqued her interest in severe weather. At the time, she was in school only a few miles away. She was amazed at how the meteorologists remained calm on TV and vowed to be that voice of calm during severe weather occurrences.

4. Shirleen Allicot

Shirleen is a co-host of the Eyewitness News this Morning and the Eyewitness News at Noon. Before that, she used to be the co-anchor of Action News at four on WPVI and Action News at ten on WPHL. She has covered many news events ranging from natural disasters to terrorism.

She was on the ground during Hurricane Sandy and the Boston Marathon bombings, and the Amtrak crash in 2015. However, the highlight of her career was probably when she interviewed First Lady Michelle Obama in the White House Kennedy Garden. She also covered the 2018 Royal Wedding in London.

5. Crystal Cranmore

Crystal Cranmore works as the Race and Culture Reporter for WABC-Eyewitness TV's News. She is a Manhattan native, even though she grew up in Maplewood, New Jersey. Returning to WABC was like a dream come true because it is her joy to share people's stories as part of the news team at Channel 7. Before joining WABC-TV, she was a reporter at KYW-TV.

Her resume includes covering breaking news, general assignment and feature pieces. In addition, she did an investigative report that resulted in reform in Philadelphia's fire escape inspection rules, and it was nominated for an Emmy Award. She also covered the presidential campaigns of 2016, among many other high-level news events.

6. Michelle Charlesworth

Weekends cannot be complete at ABC without Michelle Charlesworth. She is the reporter and co-anchor of WABC TV's famous Saturday and Sunday Morning Eyewitness News. She also has a wealth of experience, having reported from Israel and the occupied territories and the Oscars Red Carpet since 1998.

7. Lauren Glassberg

8. Sam Ryan

Her return to WABC in 2018 proved that she is an asset to the media giant. The Emmy-nominated sports anchor and reporter is one of the prominent ABC News personalities. She was WABC's weekend morning sports anchor and reporter in 2002-2003 before leaving to join ESPN, where she became a full-time sideline reporter.

She has covered several high-level sporting events for ESPN and ABC Sports, such as the NHL, Sunday Night Baseball, Stanley Cup Playoffs, Home Run Derby, College Football, Little League World Series, and the 2005 Monday Night Football broadcasts. She also worked for CBS Sports, where she covered The NFL.

9. Naveen Dhaliwal

Naveen had always dreamt of becoming a journalist from her childhood years; hence the opportunity to be one of ABC news contributors was one she was not going to miss. Her career began with a job as a speech pathologist, but since she wanted to be a journalist, she resigned and went back to school. She finally joined Eyewitness News as a reporter in February 2018.

11. Heather O’Rourke

She began reporting traffic in 1992 in the tri-state area. She was hired as an intern but quickly rose to become a full-time traffic reporter in 1994. She became part of the Eyewitness News in 2002 and started as an afternoon traffic reporter. She finally joined the morning team in January of 2013. She has also covered traffic in several radio stations in the tri-state area.

She graduated with a Bachelor of Arts degree in Journalism and Mass Media from Rutgers University. She has received four nominations for the Achievement in Radio Award for Best Traffic Reporter and eventually won it in 2001. Heather was also the recipient of the George Hammell Cook Distinguished Alumni Award by the Cook College at Rutgers University in 2012.

12. Darla Miles
