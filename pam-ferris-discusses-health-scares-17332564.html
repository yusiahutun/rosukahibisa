Call the Midwife star reveals fears that health condition would kill her

Publish date: 2024-09-06

The telly favourite, 66, said she was terrified that her condition would kill her.

Pam said: “I’ve had scares. There were moments when I was worried about what was going to happen, as people my age do.

“It can be terrifying. The prospect of a terminal illness is absolutely terrifying. It is a massive preoccupation when you’re going through something like that.

“Mercifully, no scares I’ve had have ever materialised into anything serious. But at the time it can be a hard thing to overcome.”

Fans will see Pam’s character Sister Evangelina fall ill when Call The Midwife returns for its fourth series tonight. And she said her own scares helped her do the emotional plot justice.

Pam said: “As an actor you draw on your own experiences and it is frightening playing a storyline like this. Sister Evangelina is suddenly feeling her own mortality – she’s worried this could be curtains.

“The truth is we’re all frightened of that happening. It doesn’t matter how much faith we might have, knowing we could have a serious disease isn’t easy.”

She added: “You’ll see in the new series that Sister Evangelina gets very spiky about it. And that’s because she’s scared.

“She feels that her purpose in life is blocked and she’s asking herself how she can believe in a God that would call you to do things and then make you so ill you couldn’t do them.

“There’s an internal challenge going on for her.”

The BBC1 drama, which pulls in more than ten million viewers every episode, has brought Pam a new generation of fans. She was already known to many viewers for roles in The Darling Buds of May and Rosemary & Thyme.

Pam said she would never be religious in real life.

She said: “I don’t have to be a murderer to play one. And I am an atheist. I don’t know what faith is.

“I think that actually helps me play the part.”

Since first airing three years ago Call The Midwife has become telly’s most popular drama.

The show, which also stars Miranda Hart, 42, has now had a fifth series commissioned by Beeb chiefs.

Call The Midwife is back tonight at 8pm.
