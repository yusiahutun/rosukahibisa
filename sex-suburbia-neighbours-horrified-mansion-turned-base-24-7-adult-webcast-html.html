Angry neighbours have hit out at an adult website which has overtaken a suburban Arizona home to webcast its naked male house-mates 24 hours a day. 

Fratpad.com has moved a group of men into the seven-bedroom mansion in exclusive Paradise Valley to be broadcast to adult subscribers who can also pay to chat to the men privately.

The website shows pictures of the group of up to 12 men, both naked and partially dressed, cavorting by the poolside, giving each other massages and lying in bed.

Candid camera: Adult website Fratpad.com has provoked anger in Paradise Valley, Arizona, after taking over a house to webcast its naked male house-mates to Internet subscribers

It also features a tour of the 7,500-square-foot property led by one of the naked housemates. He boasts of the luxury accommodation including seven bathrooms, a jacuzzi and a cinema room.

At one point he lies on the house's snooker table and chats and play-fights with another naked housemate in the home-cinema.

He also walks around the property's bedrooms to the soundtrack of Marvin Gaye's Let's Get It On, adding to camera: "They say this is where the magic happens, but really this is where the magic happens."

But Fratpad's arrival in Arizona hasn't only caught the attention of subscribers they are trying to encourage to tune in.

The business has also attracted the ire of nearby residents who have hit out at the 'pornography house' opened in the middle of their neighbourhood.

One homeowner has written to other residents to warn them of the activities at the 'pornography house' and to encourage them to complain to the City Council about it.

Another, Lee Morris, told AZFamily.com that he had paid a lot to live in the area and the house "should not be tolerated."

But Fratpad's owner, John Marsh, told the local news site that Fratpad was good for Paradise Valley.

'[We have] really saved that house from foreclosure. We've been taking very good care of it,' he said.

He acknowledged the website is 'adult-oriented' but added that his company has 'other website properties that are operated in other locations that are much more sexually explicit than Fratpad.'

He added: 'We're not really doing pornography. It's live webcam and very fun and silly.'

According to AZFamily, police have investigated activities at the house but haven't found any code violations.

Property prices in the affluent area are reportedly some of the highest in the state.

The median price is $1.74million.
