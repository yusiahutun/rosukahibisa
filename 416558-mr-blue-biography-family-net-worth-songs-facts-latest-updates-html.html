Early life and education

Khery Sameer Rajab, aka Mr Blue, was born on 14th April 1987 in Tanzania to Sameer Alhaji Alhad Ally and Halima Rajab. He has three siblings. He attended Prison School and completed his primary education in 1999. He later enrolled at Dar es Salaam Secondary but dropped out of school in 2004 while in Form 2.

Sameer attributes his hunger for becoming an artist as the reason why he quit attending school. From time to time, he had to sing to make money to pay school fees. Eventually, he chose music. Mr Blue's parents did not try to stop him nor force him back to school. They thought he was becoming a stubborn teenager and that he would get over the music dream.

Career milestones

Mr Blue's stage performances began in 1999. His brothers, The Boy Stain, and Obrey were also involved in the music scene. But, Khery’s debut was in 2003-2004 when he released the song ‘Mr Blue.’

Following his fame through this song, the artist began making collabos with other Bongo Flava musicians and from East Africa. His most recent partnership was with Jux and Shetta. Mr Blue's songs include:

He regrets his past behaviours

While speaking to Rwanda’s ‘The New Times,’ in 2018, Mr Blue from Tanzania opened up about the things he regrets doing. For example, he does not like his tattoos anymore, and his ear piercings remind him of decisions that he made out of mischief and peer pressure.

He also admits that he might quit music at some point to pursue other things. He further explained that when you become older, these things are no longer important. However, the artist has always been passionate about music. Concerning any past behaviour that he regrets, here is what the Tanzanian rapper had to say:

I started being mischievous a long time ago. I pierced my ears while I was still in class seven. I think I might leave singing later in life and forget about music. I do not like my tattoos and I do not enjoy seeing my ears pierced. Age tells you to stop doing some things.
