Everyone is furious with claim that yoga pants are bad for women

Publish date: 2024-08-07

The issue of yoga pants has divided the masses today after one writer claimed that to wear them is bad for women.

A New York Times opinion piece by Honor Jones argued that women only exercise in stretch leggings because "they're sexy". She asserted that tracksuit bottoms have fallen out of favour because "no one looks good in them", which doesn't fit in with (in her view) the increasingly high expectations of what a woman should look like when working out.

"We aren’t wearing these workout clothes because they’re cooler or more comfortable. (You think the selling point of Lululemon’s Reveal Tight Precision pants is really the way their moth-eaten design provides a “much-needed dose of airflow”?) We’re wearing them because they’re sexy," she said in a particularly contentious statement.

"We felt we had to look hot on dates — a given. We felt we had to look hot at the office — problematic," she continued. "But now we’ve internalised the idea that we have to look hot at the gym? Give me a break. The gym is one of the few places where we’re supposed to be able to focus on how our bodies feel, not just on how they look. We need to remember that. Sweatpants can help."

She argued that form-fitting yoga pants have little to do with being able to exercise better: "Are practitioners really worried that a normal-width pant leg is going to throttle them mid-lotus pose?"

Unsurprisingly, the article has riled many on social media.
