Early life

Rozonda was born in Atlanta, Georgia, on February 27, 1971, to Ava Thomas and Abdul Ali. She was raised in the States by her single mother and attended Benjamin E. Mays High School. Unfortunately, her parents broke up before she was born, so she didn't get to see her father for 25 years.

What is Rozonda Chilli Thomas' ethnicity?

Her mother, Ava, is of African and Native American ancestry, while her father, Abdul Ali, is of East Indian and Middle Eastern origin. As a result, she is racially diverse. Rozonda Thomas' father was drafted into the army and was stationed at the Columbus, Georgia military post.

She only got to meet her father after the Sally Jessy Raphael Show producer tracked him down. He was working at an Atlantic City casino at the time. Since she was a child, she had a passion for dancing and worked as a backup dancer for the R&B duo Damian Dame, who was signed to LaFace Records.

Personal life

Her love life began with Dallas Austin, a well-known American rapper and film producer. She became pregnant with the rapper at a young age, but she terminated the pregnancy. The couple, however, had a son named Tron Austin in June 1997. Tron wished to be a musician in the same way that his parents did. Unfortunately, Rozonda broke up with Dallas before they could marry.

After some time, Chilli was spotted with Usher Raymond, a famous musician, in 2001. On October 22, 2013, when speaking with Bet, she admitted that Usher was her true love. However, their romance ended in 2003. Two years later, Rozonda claimed he was cheating on her.

In 2014, she dated comedian Wayne Brady. The TLC singer began dating actor Brad James in 2015. But, as with her previous relationship, she ended things with him quickly.

She later got into a relationshipwith American rapper Nick Cannon. According to Daily Mail, the couple was caught kissing backstage at the WDKX Summerfest Concert on July 14, 2016. Unfortunately, that relationship did not work out either.

Career

Thomas started as a dancer. In 1991, she joined TLC as a replacement for Crystal Jones. With over 65 million albums sold globally, the band was a hit. Chilli earned four Grammy Awards for her contributions to music.

In the year 2000, she began work on a solo record, but it was never completed. She then went on to record several singles. Unfortunately, the band was disbanded in 2002 due to the death of Lisa Lopez, a founding member.

Tyrese's music video for his track Nothing on You included her as the starring lady in 2012. In addition, Chilli worked in the VH1 biopic CrazySexyCool: The TLC Story as an executive producer in 2013. The show was about TLC's peak and fall in popularity.
