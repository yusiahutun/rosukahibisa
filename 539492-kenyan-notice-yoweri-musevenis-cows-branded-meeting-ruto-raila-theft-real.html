Kenyans Notice Yoweri Museveni's Cows Are Branded During Meeting with Ruto, Raila: &quot;Theft Is Real&quot;

Publish date: 2024-02-12
  • Uganda's president Yoweri Museveni hosted his Kenyan counterpart William Ruto and opposition chief Raila Odinga on Monday
  • The agenda of the meeting revolved around issues pertinent to Nairobi and Kampala as Ruto revealed
  • In one of the photos that the politician shared, Larry Madowo noticed some of Yoweri Museveni's cows were branded

Kampala - Kenya's president William Ruto has engaged with the country's opposition chief, Raila Odinga, in a meeting in Uganda on Monday, February 26.

President Yoweri Museveni hosted the duo.

Ruto revealed that on top of the agenda was boosting the diplomatic ties between Nairobi and Kampala and the need to bolster the relationship between all seven constituting East African Community nations.

He hinted at the countries uniting under the East African Political Federation to champion their interests.

Why are Museveni's cows branded?

The president looked at Museveni's cows, which Kenyans noticed were branded.

After photos of the trio went viral, CNN reporter Larry Madowo tweeted:

"President M7’s cows are branded YKM - Yoweri Kaguta Museveni."

Kenyans reacting to the images wondered whether cattle theft was so rampant that Uganda's president had to brand his cows.

In 2021, The Daily Monitor Uganda reported that three people were arrested for stealing Museveni's cows

What Raila Ruto discussed with Museveni

The discussions delved into various matters, particularly emphasising Raila's AU bid.

Museveni staunchly supported Raila's candidacy, adding to the momentum generated by Ruto's endorsement after Raila declared his interest in the bid.

"Crucially, at the urging of President Museveni, we also discussed my candidacy for Chairperson of the African Union Commission. I am very grateful to President Museveni for strongly endorsing my candidacy and President Ruto for fully backing it," he disclosed.
