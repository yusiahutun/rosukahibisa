NIMHANS Secures 4th Position in NIRF Indian Rankings (2023)

Publish date: 2024-08-13

The National Institute of Mental Health and Neurosciences (NIMHANS) has achieved a great feat by ranking fourth among the best medical schools in India in the National Institutional Ranking Framework (NIRF) rankings for 2023. This outstanding acknowledgement strengthens NIMHANS’ dedication to excellence in the area of mental health and neurosciences.

NIRF Ranking

The Ministry of Education, Government of India, releases the NIRF rankings each year as a thorough evaluation of educational institutions in a variety of fields. The outstanding success of NIMHANS demonstrates its commitment to offering top-notch clinical care, clinical research, and education in the field of mental health and neurosciences.

The fourth place in the NIRF rankings honours both NIMHANS’ significant societal achievements as well as its academic prowess. The institute has produced innumerable professionals. They are improving the lives of people impacted by mental health issues by promoting a supportive and inclusive environment.

NIMHANS

The National Institute of Mental Health and Neurosciences is referred to as NIMHANS. It is a prestigious medical facility in Bangalore, India. For its knowledge and contributions to the disciplines of mental health and neuroscience, NIMHANS is well-known.

Since its founding in 1925, NIMHANS has made significant contributions to the fields of mental health and neurological illnesses in terms of research, instruction, and patient care. The facility provides a wide variety of specialised treatments, such as rehabilitation, neurology consultations, neurosurgery, neuroimaging, and neuropsychology, among others.

Agenda of NIMHANS

NIMHANS dedicates itself to enhancing mental health, treating neurological diseases, and increasing our understanding of the human brain. It continues to have an impact on the future of mental health and neurosciences.

NIMHANS aims to make a significant contribution to mental health policies and advocacy. It actively influences the national agenda for mental health. It aims to advance holistic approaches to mental health and neurosciences. These approaches cover prevention, early intervention, and comprehensive care and involve collaboration with governmental and non-governmental organizations.

The NIMHANS institute is inspired and motivated to keep up its unrelenting pursuit of excellence. This drive is fueled by the acclaim conferred upon it in the NIRF rankings.

The objective of NIMHANS is to advance mental health research and deliver compassionate care. Additionally, it aims to promote a culture that values and promotes mental health.

Criteria for NIRF Ranking

Indian educational institutions are assessed using a variety of factors by the NIRF (National Institutional assessment Framework) assessment. The following factors go into NIRF ranking:

  • Resources, Teaching, and Learning (TLR): This criterion evaluates the effectiveness and calibre of teaching strategies, the faculty-to-student ratio, and the accessibility of tools like libraries, labs, and digital infrastructure. It also assesses the general learning environment.
  • Research and Professional Practise (RP): This criterion assesses the institution’s research production and impact, including the quantity of research publications, patent applications, joint ventures, and financed research projects. It also takes into account the involvement of professors in consulting and executive development programmes as well as their participation in professional activities.
  • Graduation Outcomes (GO): This criterion evaluates educational outcomes, including graduation rates and students’ employability, placement rates, and success in postsecondary education or entrepreneurship. Additionally, it considers graduates’ median salaries as well as how well-regarded the institution is in their eyes.
  • The outreach and inclusiveness (OI): criterion evaluates the institution’s efforts to advance diversity, inclusivity, and social outreach. The institution considers factors including the percentage of women and members of socially disadvantaged groups among students and academics, programming for underserved populations, and initiatives intended to aid them.
  • Peer Perception: This criterion gathers the opinions of academic peers, including deans, department heads, and faculty from other institutions. They provide insights on the academic reputation, programme excellence, and research output of the institution.
  • Graduation Outcome Perception: The institution conducts surveys of both employers and alumni to understand how they perceive the institution’s graduates in terms of knowledge, abilities, and employability. This helps the institution gain insights into the viewpoints of these two important groups.
  • Public Perception: This criterion considers how the broader public—including students, parents, and other stakeholders—perceives the institution’s standing, the calibre of its programmes, and its social contribute

NIMHANS has earned a well-deserved reputation as a premier organization in mental health and neurosciences. This is due to its persistent focus on cutting-edge research, patient-centric care, and community outreach initiatives. Teachers, staff, and students are recognised for their unshakable dedication and diligence in the NIRF rankings.
