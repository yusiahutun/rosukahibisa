A 14-year-old boy who helped to kill his friend's 78-year-old great grandmother with a hatchet broke down sobbing in court as he was sentenced to life in jail.

Nathan Paape has been sentenced to life in prison for his role in the murder of Barbara Olson despite pleading he only took part in the brutal slaying so his friend Antonio Barbeau, also 14, would not turn on him. The family will appeal the sentence.

A family member pleaded after the hearing that Paape is developmentally challenged and was only eager to please a friend.

'In my 24 years on the bench, I’ve not seen anything of this nature. Not even close,' he said during his ruling.

A family member speaking to local media also apologized for the grisly murder.

'I know this is bad, really bad. And no one can bring Barbara Olson back and our family. We haven’t had the opportunity to express our sympathy for their family,' great aunt Lorrie Keone told Fox 6.

Keone added that her great-nephew has the 'social development of a 10-year-old and would easily be led by a smarter boy such as Antonio Barbeau.'

As further proof of the teen's developmental challenges, Keone told the station that Paape didn't think he would be in trouble after confessing to the crime.

'He actually thought he was going to get to come home after he told the truth,” Koene told Fox 6, adding that 'he didn’t want this to happen. He didn’t plan to go there to kill Barbara Olson. He didn’t plan this.'

That mental issue was not enough to disregard the brutal nature of the murder.

'There’s just so much sorrow for everybody involved, so much sorrow,' Keone added.

That mental issue was not enough to disregard the brutal nature of the murder.

'I feel bad for the family. I know they love him. But this type of crime cannot go unanswered and be explained away by brain trauma,' district attorney Joe DeCecco said.

'The kid’s life is gone. He has to wait until he’s 50. He won’t be anything like he is today. But that’s the way it is.'

Paape's family plans to appeal both the sentence and the charges being tried in an adult court, Keone told Fox 6.

'Maybe the laws will change when it comes to sentencing kids. I hope it does. I hope it does,' Koene said to Fox 6.

Victim: Barbara Olson, 78, was found in a pool of blood at her home last September

The Paape's account of the savage murder was far different from Barbeau's, the Shaboygan Press reported.

Paape claimed the robbery and murder were Barbeau's idea and that he only hit Olson twice with a hammer because he feared his friend would turn on him.

Paape thought Barbeau was joking about killing Olson, his lawyer said. But Barbeau said his friend never suggested he thought it was a joke.

Olson's body was found in a pool of blood outside her home last September.

Once the teens realized Olson was dead after the horrific attack, they attempted to drag her body to the car, leaving a trail on blood through the house, police said last September.

After failing to load Olson into the vehicle, they dumped her in the garage.

The boys then allegedly stole several items, including a purse, loose change and jewelry.

The perpetrators then hopped into Olson's car and sped off. They eventually dumped the vehicle in the parking lot of a local bowling alley and walked to a nearby eatery, where they shared a pizza.

Barbeau was also sentenced to life in prison for his role in the crime. His sentence was handed down Monday.
