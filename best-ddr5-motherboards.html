Best DDR5 Motherboards 2023

Publish date: 2024-05-03

DDR5 is slowly going mainstream, with both Intel and AMD now supporting it on their latest CPUs and chipsets. Upgrading to these new memory kits means better performance and efficiency for your PC, as DDR5 boasts faster speeds—nearly twice as quick as DDR4—while consuming less power.

Unfortunately, DDR5 isn't backward compatible, meaning you'll need a new DDR5-compatible motherboard to take the new chips for a spin. Intel DDR5 motherboards have been out for a while since the release of the 12th Gen CPUs, and you now have options for AMD, too, with the new AM5 platform.

Plenty of mid-range Alder Lake motherboards support DDR5 memory, but MSI's MPG Z690 CARBON WIFI offers a few features that make it our favorite for building a mainstream gaming PC. Finding the Best DDR5 Motherboard For You

When it comes to searching for a DDR5-capable motherboard, there are a few factors to consider before committing to a purchase. First, you should know if the motherboard and the rest of your PC parts are compatible. It doesn't matter if you can achieve flagship performance from your CPU if it won't fit in the socket on your motherboard. Next, you'll want to scan the board for any features you're after, such as a BIOS flashback button or a 2.5G Ethernet port. Lastly, you'll want to make sure the price tag fits your budget.

If you're after the best of the best for most situations, the ASUS ROG Maximus Z790 Hero is the pick for you. Not only does it support DDR5 memory beautifully, but it also provides Thunderbolt 4 ports for fast charging from your PC, two PCIe 5.0 x16 slots, Wi-Fi 6e, a BIOS flashback button, and a slack all-black design with RGB trim.
