Ciara biography

The singer was born on 25th October 1985 on the Fort Hood Military Base in Killeen, Texas. Her full name is Ciara Princess Wilson. She was named after the Revlon fragrance, Ciara, which was introduced in 1973 and still sold to date.

Ciara's parents are Jackie and Carlton Clay Harris. Who are Ciara's siblings? The singer does not have any siblings.

Being the child of military personnel, the singer and her family relocated to different places such as New York City, Germany, Utah, California, Nevada and Arizona. During her early teenagehood, Ciara and her parents settled in Atlanta, Georgia.

Singing career

Ciara started singing in her mid-teens when she formed an all-girl music group known as the Hearsay alongside two of her friends. The group recorded demos but later split after experiencing differences amongst the members. Even with the disbandment, she was determined to achieve her goal and thus signed a publishing deal as a songwriter.

In 2003, the singer was signed by L. A Reid, LaFace Records executive. She began working on her debut album, Goodies, released on 28th September 2004. The album attained the following:

  • It debuted at number three on the US Billboard 200.
  • It sold over 120K copies in its initial weeks of release.
  • It topped the Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums chart.
  • It earned her the title, First Lady of Crunk&B.
  • The album had a 71- week run on the Billboard 200.
  • On 10th October 2006, the album was certified three times-platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America.
  • As of June 2010, the album had sold close to 3 million copies in the United States.
  • It charted at number 22 on the Canadian Albums Chart.
  • It was certified platinum by the Canadian Recording Industry Association.
  • The album charted at number 26 on the UK Albums Chart and stayed 20 weeks on the chart.
  • It was certified Silver by the British Phonographic Industry, and to date, it remains her only UK certified album.
