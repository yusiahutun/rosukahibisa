Early Life & Family

Jrue Holiday is one of the four kids of former basketball professionals Shawn Holiday and Toya Holiday.

The brilliant basketball player Jrue Holiday was born in Los Angeles on June 12, 1990. 

Holiday grew up in Los Angeles, California, and has been blessed with three siblings, Justin, Aaron, and Lauren Holiday. It’s a coincidence that his sister’s name matches his wife’s.

His parents played basketball at Arizona State, where his mother, Toya, was named 1982 Pac-10 Player of the Year.

Likewise, his brothers are also NBA players. His oldest brother, Justin Holiday, is a professional basketball player for the Indiana Pacers, whereas Aaron Holiday is an experienced player for the Washington Wizards.

Jrue attended Campbell High School and UCLA College from 2008 to 2009. Previously, Holiday was interested in playing football and did great in the same realm.

After he lost the football championship, he switched his preference to basketball.

Basketball Career

High School Career

Jrue Holiday attended Campbell Hall School in Studio City, California. As a senior team member, he notched 25.9 points, 11.2 Rebounds, 6.9 assists, and 4.8 steals per game.

Therefore, in 2008, he was rated the No. 1 point guard and No. 2 overall prospect in the class by Rivals.com.

He was named a Parade first-team All-American and the 2008 Gatorade National Player of the Year. 

Similarly, Holiday also played in the McDonald’s All-American Game, averaging 14 points, five rebounds, three assists, and five steals.

College Career

In 2008-2009, Holiday played with Darren Collision (future NBA player) at UCLA.

He shot 45% from the ground, 30.7% from the 3-point range, and 72.6% from the line.

With his best performance, he earned Pac-10 All-Freshman team honors.

After his sophomore year at UCLA, he declared for the NBA Draft, forgoing his final three years of college eligibility.

Philadelphia 76ers

Jrue Holiday was drafted in the 2009 NBA Draft by the Philadelphia 76ers with the 17th overall pick.

He recorded his first triple-double with 11 points, 10 rebounds, and 11 assists against the Nets on November 5, 2010.

He was appointed as a reserve for the Eastern Conference All-Star team for the 2013 NBA All-Star Game.

The 22-year-old Jrue Holiday became the youngest player in the franchise’s history to be named All-Star.

New Orleans Pelicans

Holiday was traded to New Orleans Pelicans for Nerlens Noel and the Pelicans’ 2014 first-round draft pick in July 2013. 

He reached the 5,000 career points mark in January 2015 while playing against the Wizards.

On February 18, he was ruled out for three weeks after aggravating an already injured lower right leg.

He made a comeback to action much later than expected, coming off the bench against the Suns on April 10 after missing 41 games.

Similarly, on March 29, he was ruled out for the rest of the season after being diagnosed with a right interior orbital wall fracture.

On November 18, 2016, Holiday made his season debut after missing the first 12 games while he was on leave to take care of his wife.

Holiday extended his stay with the team in July 2017.

On March 26, 2018, he underwent season-ending surgery to repair a core muscle injury.

Furthermore, on December 28, 2019, Jrue, Justin, and Aaron became the first trio of brothers to share an NBA court simultaneously in the Pelicans-Pacers game.

Milwaukee Bucks

Jrue Holiday was traded to Milwaukee Bucks in a four-team trade, including Oklahoma City Thunder and Denver Nuggets. 

He scored 15 points in 23 minutes of action and hit the victory with an 11-foot jump shot in a match against the Grizzlies in March 2021.

On his way to an NBA First Team All-Defense selection, Jrue averaged a team-high 1.63 steals per game for the Bucks, which was fifth-best in the league. 

Moreover, he made multiple steals in around 32 games, the second-most highest score of any basketball player over the season.

In Game 6 of the Conference Finals against the Hawks, Holiday dropped 27 points, 9 rebounds, and 9 assists in a 118–107 win, leading Milwaukee to the NBA Finals for the first time since 1974.

Holiday scored 27 points, 13 assists, and four rebounds in Game 5 of the NBA Finals against the Phoenix Suns.

Similarly, he recorded 12 points, 11 assists, and 9 rebounds to help the Bucks gain victory over the Suns and win the 2021 NBA Championship.

The man is still with the Bucks, and his gameplay has only improved. On February 14, 2023, Jrue tied a career-high 40 points, with a career-high eight three-point shots made, during a 131–125 overtime win against the Celtics.

Net Worth

The Bucks’ point guard, Jrue Holiday, has a net worth of $30 million as of November 2023.

In 2017, he signed a 5-year/$126 million contract with the Pelicans with an average annual salary of $25 million.

Similarly, on April 4, 2021, he inked a 4-year/$135 million contract extension with the Bucks, including the full amount guaranteed and an annual average salary of $33.74 million.

NBA salaries are not the only thing that Jrue pockets, his endorsement deals with Carnival Crop, SeatGeek, Panini, and Smoothie King also flow a good amount of money to his bank account.

Likewise, during his time with the Pelicans, Holiday signed a multi-year footwear and apparel endorsement deal with Nike.

Jrue Holiday Charity Foundation

Jrue and his wife, Lauren Holiday, operate non-profit organizations with the objective of uplifting communities.

The pair is committed to flourishing communities by creating significant change when and where needed.

Personal Life

The Bucks’ point guard Jrue Holiday married Lauren Holiday in July 2013.

Jrue and Lauren first met in college. They both played sports for UCLA, and both of them are extensively competitive.

Lauren is a former professional soccer player for the United States women’s national team.

She is a two-time gold medalist in the 2008 and 2012 Summer Olympics and won the 2015 Women’s World Cup.

In September 2016, Lauren was diagnosed with a brain tumor during her third month of pregnancy.

Jrue took a leave from NBA for his wife’s brain surgery. After his family felt secure, safe, and happy, he made his comeback to the league.

After the delivery of her first child, the tumor was successfully removed.

She gave birth to a beautiful baby girl named Tyler Holiday on September 20, 2016. 

They welcomed their second child, a baby boy named Hendricks, in late 2020.
