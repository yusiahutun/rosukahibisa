New father Dance meets with old flame within 48 hours of daughter's birth

By Richard Kay for the Daily Mail

Published: 22:05 EST, 11 March 2012 | Updated: 10:41 EST, 13 March 2012

18

comments

Veteran actor Charles Dance will have to set aside any thoughts he may have cherished of slowing down into a comfortable retirement. He has just became a father again at 65.

Eligible to draw his state pension since last October, Dance was last week celebrating the latest addition to his family — a 6lb 3oz daughter by his girlfriend of the past four years, painter and sculptress Eleanor Boorman, who is 26 years his junior.

But yesterday, just six days after the baby was born, the Jewel In The Crown star was jetting off to South Africa on a work assignment. Indeed, he couldn’t be at the birth at London’s Chelsea and Westminster Hospital. 

Still close: Mr Dance and Shambhala Marthe were together last week

‘Charles was filming,’ says Eleanor, ‘but he came down straight away. He has helped with the nursery and bought a plethora of lovely things.’ 

However, other new mothers might be less forgiving at the idea of the father of their baby taking another woman out within 48 hours of the birth.

Missed out: Mr Dance was not at the hospital for his daughter's birth

For Dance, I can reveal, was spotted lunching  with his former companion, French-born ex-Gucci model Shambhala Marthe, 36.

He and 6ft 2in tall Shambhala spent an hour at the Kalendar restaurant in Hampstead, North London, and afterwards drove off together in his car.

Meanwhile, Eleanor, who has named the baby Rose Otilie Aaryanna, tells me: ‘Mother and baby are doing very well. I am now in Cheltenham with the baby but I have met Shambhala in the past. No, she hasn’t seen the baby, who is absolutely gorgeous and a delight — only Charles and my family have.’  At 39, Eleanor is not much older than Dance’s other children Oliver, 36, and Rebecca, 31, from his 33-year former marriage to Joanna Haythorn, also a sculptress.

He separated from Joanna in 2004 and became involved with Thunderbirds actress Sophia Myles, then aged just 25.

Eleanor is the granddaughter of wildlife artist Harry Pettit. Educated at Princess Anne’s old school, Benenden, she has painted portraits of the Royal Family and Prince Charles describes her work as ‘marvellous’.

One of her latest works is a bust of the evergreen Mr Dance.

 

Retired Voice of Racing returns to Cheltenham

At the age of 94, Sir Peter O’Sullevan will be driving himself down to Cheltenham today for the National Hunt festival. The retired Voice of Racing, who first motored to Cheltenham 77 years ago and has attended the festivals ever since, will have his fingers crossed about congestion en route. ‘With the volume of traffic we have today it was quicker to drive from London on race days in 1935 than it is now,’ he informs me.

 

The secret joy of MP Skinner

Determined: Labour MP Dennis Skinner

Despite making a full recovery from cancer and turning 80 last month, Labour MP Dennis Skinner has not mellowed sufficiently to agree to participate in a Diamond Jubilee celebration of old age.

Told that fellow contributors included Sir Patrick Moore, Sandy Gall, Baroness Trumpington and fellow Labour stalwart Lord Healey, Skinner declared: ‘I don’t want to be involved with anything to do with the Royal Family. I don’t go in for this silly celebration.

‘I’m not interested in happy birthdays. I’m not interested in this caper.’

Barely drawing breath, the Beast of Bolsover boomed: ‘I’m not being miserable. With happiness sometimes you turn a corner and there it is.

‘When I had wonderful cancer treatment and I came out of the Chelsea and Westminster Hospital and saw that wonderful blossom, I was floating on air with happiness. So don’t tell me I don’t know what happiness is. Royalty doesn’t make me laugh.’

 

Patrick Cox's dreams of being best man

Swanky cobbler Patrick Cox is keeping his fingers crossed he will be called upon when Liz Hurley says ‘I do’ to her glossy Aussie beau Shane Warne. ‘I’d love to be best man,’ he tells me at Nobu restaurant on Park Lane. ‘Trouble is Elton [John] might get asked first!’ Meanwhile, Cox, a victim of conman Eddie Davenport, says talk of his business demise is exaggerated.

‘I’ve been though a rough patch, but not as bad as people assumed,’ he says. ‘I bought a Banksy print at a charity auction recently and even Kate Moss came up and said: “I thought you were broke!” ’

 

Young one for granny Saunders

Happy news: Singer Ella

She’s faced a tough couple of years after being diagnosed with breast cancer and then suffering from depression, but there are happier times ahead for comedian Jennifer Saunders, 53.

For folk singer Ella, the eldest of her three daughters by fellow comedian husband Ade Edmondson, is to make Jennifer a first-time grandmother.

Blonde Ella, 26, who lives with her builder husband of a year-and-a-half Daniel Furlong in a restored three-bedroom cottage in Dartmoor, is due to give birth in August.

Indeed, for someone who has worked as a barmaid and painter and decorator before succeeding as a singer, motherhood should be a piece of cake.

‘OK, so there’s a reason you haven’t got much outta (sic) me on the music front. Truth is I’ve been making something else... a baby! 16 weeks today,’ she tweeted.

Says a friend: ‘Jennifer is over the moon at the prospect of becoming a grandmother.’

 

PS Might actress Diana Quick undergo a spot of cosmetic surgery? Once hailed the most beautiful woman in the world, photographed by Cecil Beaton, David Bailey and Lord Snowdon, she admits it crosses her mind.

‘One can’t help having those thoughts, especially if you’re staring at a mirror at six o’clock in the morning,’ says Diana, 65, at a Women’s Day gala at the Southbank. ‘There is a great deal of pressure for women to look unlined.’ Yet she says men (her ex, sleek actor Bill Nighy, is 62 and still referred to as ‘crumpet’) are given a much easier ride. ‘It is a real double standard,’ she opines.
