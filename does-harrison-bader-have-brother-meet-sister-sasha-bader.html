Does Harrison Bader Have Brother? Meet Sister Sasha Bader

Fans are curious about the whereabouts of Harrison Bader’s brother. He has a sister named Sasha Bader. Does Harrison Bader have a brother? Read the article till the end to find out.  

Harrison Joseph Bader, a professional American baseball player, was born on June 3, 1994, in Bronxville, New York, U.S.

He is currently playing center field for the New York Yankees of Major League Baseball (MLB). Previously, he played for the St. Louis Cardinals in MLB.

The player started to play baseball during college at the University of Florida. After being selected in the third round of the 2015 MLB draft by the Cardinals, he made his MLB debut in 2017.

Bader played five seasons as their starting center fielder. The star athlete won the Gold Glove Award in 2021.

However, in 2022, Harrison was traded to the Yankees by the Cardinals.

After that, the player initially played for Team Israel in the World Baseball Classic in 2023. Unfortunately, he couldn’t play due to his injury.

Harrison Bader Brother and Siblings

Harrison doesn’t have a brother but a sister. Bader grew up with his sister, Sasha Bader. They were beautifully raised by Louis and Janice Bader. 

Sasha Bader, the player’s younger sister, was born on October 21, 1998. She graduated from the Horace Mann School in 2017.

Sasha joined the Bachelor of Arts, BA, Psychology program in 2017 and graduated in 2021 from Lafayette College.

During her college years, the younger sister started to work as an intern at Mosaic Mental Health, and after that, she worked as a part-timer for 3 months at Whim Boutique in 2019.

Again in 2020, Sasha did an internship for 3 months at the New York Grays Baseball Club, Inc. In 2021, she joined Tierney for one month as an intern.

 In addition to that, after her graduation, she started to do full-time work for Publicis Groupe as a strategy associate for almost 7 months.

Currently, Sasha has been working as a full-time assistant teacher at Horace Mann School since 2022.

Harrison Bader’s Career & Injuries

Harrison Bader was selected in the third round of the 2015 MLB Draft by the St. Louis Cardinals and later signed a contract. He also received a bonus worth $400,000.

Not to mention, Bader debuted with a minor league Class-A short-season affiliate team, the State College Spikes of the Cardinals.

After a year, in the 2016 season, Harrison played for the Springfield Cardinals of the Texas League, Class AA Minor League Baseball (MiLB).

After he scored four hits, including a double and a run, the player was recognized as an outfielder in the mid-season All-Star Game of the Texas League.

On July 25, 2017, Brader was promoted as the replacement for the injured Dexter Fowler in the major leagues by the Cardinals.

Again, on April 3, 2018, Harrison was replaced by an injured infielder by the Cardinals in the major leagues.

Additionally, during spring training in 2021, Bader strained his right forearm and had to miss the start of the regular season.
