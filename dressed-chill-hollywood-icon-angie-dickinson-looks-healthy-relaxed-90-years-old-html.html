Dressed to chill! Hollywood icon Angie Dickinson looks healthy and relaxed at 90 years old

Publish date: 2024-06-13

Hollywood icon Angie Dickinson is looking healthy and relaxed as ever as she stepped out to pick up the mail.  

DailyMail.com spotted the Dressed to Kill actress last week - the day before her 90th birthday - in Beverly Hills wearing a gray hat, vintage Guess sweatshirt with 'Why Not Wear The Best' on the front and sneakers. 

This is the first time the nonagenarian has been spotted in public in over three and a half years.

Angie also had a cameo in George Clooney's 2001 reboot of the Las Vegas heist movie.

Born in North Dakota as Angelina Brown, she went on to enjoy a colorful love life that included a reported ten-year affair with Frank Sinatra plus trysts with the iconic singer's Rat Pack pal Dean Martin and President John F. Kennedy.  

She was married twice, first to Gene Dickinson from 1952 to 1960 then composer Burt Bacharach between 1965 and 1981.  

However Angie's life took a tragic twist when their daughter Nikki Bacharach, who was her only child, committed suicide in 2007 aged 40.

Angie's last film performance was in 2004's Elvis Has Left The Building and she was last seen on TV in 2009's Mending Fences.

However she has appeared in a number of documentaries and news shows since then including last year's PBS documentary about another of Sinatra's Rat Pack pals Sammy Davis, JR.: I've Gotta Be Me.

Back in 2010 when she was 79, Angie reflected on her life and career telling NJ.com: 'I was lucky.

'I came along at the right time for so many great things. I met some great stars and stayed friends with many and lovers with some. It was a fantastic life, really. And it still is.'
