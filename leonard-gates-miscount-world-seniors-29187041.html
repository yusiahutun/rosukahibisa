Leonard Gates' horrific miscount at World Seniors Darts leaves commentators wincing

Publish date: 2024-09-25

Leonard Gates cruised into the second round of the World Seniors Darts Championship on Friday afternoon but not before a horrific miscount left the commentators wincing.

Nicknamed The Soulger, Gates is a much-loved member of the darting community but his maths skills have left a lot to be desired - as they did when needing 77 against Tony O'Shea.

While winning 1-0 in sets and 2-0 in legs, the Texan hit the single 19 when aiming for 57 to leave double 10, then stepped back while looking visibly confused.

Lead commentator Chris Murphy said: "So it's 18 for tops. You are allowed to ask, Leonard."

With former PDC star Paul Nicholson saying on commentary after the second dart, which was clearly not aimed at 18 which would've left tops, hit the treble three: "Oh no! Everything was wrong with that dart."

Murphy continued: "Well everything was wrong with the whole process wasn't it, he wasn't sure because he had a look up at the screen and he didn't ask. You are allowed to ask the referee."

Gates is appearing at the World Seniors after playing at the PDC World Championships in late 2022, having charmed fans with his reaction to qualifying.

"After the dust settles and I know I'm going… I'm not going to worry about who I'm playing first, just go and play darts, keep my game the same and go from there,” he told Online Darts TV.

"When it sinks in, I’m going to be by myself, I might be out in the woods and I'll just be, 'yeah, I made it!' I’m going to soak it in real good.

"I’m just going to go forward and play the greatest darts that I can play and hope they fall into place when they need to fall into place."

Gates nailed the highest checkout of the tournament so far with 164 to book a second round tie against Martin Adams, the losing finalist from 2022.

Other stars in action include Phil Taylor and Keith Deller, with the latter causing some controversy at the tournament with his use of chalk.
