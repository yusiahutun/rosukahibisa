Who is Bijou Phillips? 

Bijou Phillips is an American actress, model and singer - her most notable film appearances include 'Almost Famous,' 'Bully' and 'Hostel: Part II'.

She was born in Greenwich, Connecticut, on 1 April 1980, however neither of her biological parents raised her.

Bijou is the daughter of John Phillips, Mamas & The Papas musician and his third wife Geneviève Waïte. 

As both parents were deemed unfit to take care of her, she was in New York's foster care system from age three.

In a 2018 Instagram post, she shared a snap of her and her foster father Derry and her foster sister Rita.

She explained that her parents 'spent years and years fighting for custody' so she 'bounced around a lot.'

In the caption she wrote 'I’m still really close with my foster family and love them so much. I’m going home to see them this summer. 

'When I was a kid separated from my parents, I remember my mother coming and wanting to take me and just wanting to go with her so much and screaming and crying and griping the door.

'I was dragged inside it took two adults and the door closed. I didn't see her again for months.'

It was dropped when the company provided proof that all the models were adults.

At this time in her life, she became a 'wild child' who drank and took drugs including coke, ecstasy and heroin, according to the Observer.

The then 21-year-old Bijou told the publication in 2002 'I was 14 years old. What was I supposed to do?

'If you were 14 years old and able to live on your own in an apartment in New York City, and you got invited to all these clubs, and you got a bank account and you had a car service you could call so that you could go wherever you wanted... what would happen?'

Entering the world of acting, she landed her first major film in 1999 for 'Black and White,' aged 19.

It was followed by a wave of other roles in films such as 'Choke' and 'The Door to the Floor' - she notably starred in the TV series 'Raising Hope' from 2010 to 2013. 

However, on the set of the film 'Bully' which was released in 2001, Bijou was accused of 'body-shaming' and 'homophobia' and 'physical abuse' of her co-star Daniel Franzese, who's best known for his role as Damien in 'Mean Girls'.

He claimed she had taunted him for his sexuality, twisted his nipple and kicked him in the back of the head on the set of 'Bully'.

The actor later wrote on Twitter that Phillips had apologized for her actions and said that he forgave her, writing: 'I forgive because I don’t know her private story either. I shared this story only to bring light to a dark memory.'

How long have the pair been together?

Bijou met Danny Masterton at a poker tournament in Las Vegas in 2004, almost twenty years ago.

Five years later they were engaged and the couple got married in 2011 - they lived in a lavish mansion in Los Angeles.

In 2017, Masterton shared that his wife had been secretly battling a rare kidney condition for years.

One of her friends was a match and donated her kidney to the star.

Masterton thanked the 'angel' who gave his wife 'the gift of an encore' in an Instagram post that showed the couple holding hands across hospital beds.

Both Masterton and his wife are part of the Church of Scientology, as were the women that he raped.

During the sentencing Bijou sat with amid a group of Scientology friends and family members.
