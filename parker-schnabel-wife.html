Parker Schnabel Is An American Miner

Parker Schnabel is an American gold miner and a television personality known for Gold Rush. 

He started his mining career with his grandfather when he was two years old. His grandfather owned John's Big Nugget Mine in Haines, Alaska. At the age of 18, he decided to take land on lease from Tony Beets at Klondike to start his mining career. And by the age of 24, he had mined over $13 million worth of gold.

Over the years, he collaborated with Gold Rush and traveled from the historical Chilkoot Trail of the Klondike Gold Rush to Guyana and Papua New Guinea for mining.

Parker has been such a fan favorite that he acquired his very own spinoff show, Gold Rush: Parker's Trail, in 2017. During Parker's Trail's fourth season, which aired in 2020, the 27-year-old miner took an 8,000-mile ride to the giant terrains of Australia with Tyler Mahoney.

In Gold Rush's new season 13, he tirelessly searches for gold as he extends his operation from the Klondike to Alaska. Gold Rush returns September 30 at eight p.m. ET/PT on Discovery and Discovery+.
