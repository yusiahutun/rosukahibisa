Mabati house designs in Kenya: 10 stylish yet cheap starter homes

Publish date: 2024-09-12

What comes to your mind when you hear of mabati housing? Most envision poorly built structures unsafe for occupation. But if you thought that was all mabati had to offer, you are in for a pleasant surprise. Mabati house designs in Kenya have evolved into stylish pieces of architecture.

Mabati is the Swahili word for corrugated iron sheeting, mainly used as a roofing building material. But because of the negative notion associated with iron sheet houses, most people prefer to build semi-permanent structures using alternatives like wood or mud. However, thanks to the innovation of the internet, many people are being exposed to the product's potential.

Mabati house designs in Kenya

The corrugated iron sheets, better known as mabati in East Africa, have many advantages. The product is affordable, readily available, durable, and can withstand fire and harsh weather conditions. In addition, mabati is versatile and requires little to no maintenance when installed.

The benefits of mabati have propelled it as the go-to product for many when building cheap housing. But who said cheap has to be ugly? According to Construction Kenya and similar websites, here are ten stylish mabati house designs in Kenya to choose from.

1. Two bedrooms rural design

The rural cabin plan is among the best mabati house designs in Kenya. Simple yet functional, the home comprises durable corrugated iron sheets, which are easy to install. The two windows on the front provide enough lighting and add a touch of classic charm to the cabin.

2. Half brick, half mabati

The half-brick mabati house concept utilises various materials to produce a trendy and affordable modern cabin. The design is ideal for wet regions where water could erode the base of the iron sheet. The result is a stylish modern cottage that is durable and affordable.

3. Three-bedroom modern house

If you are looking for modern mabati house designs in Kenya, what better one than the three-bedroom iron sheet home? The cabin comprises corrugated iron sheets, wood, brick and mortar (for the foundation). The end product is a stunning three-bedroom modern home ideal for a large family setting.

4. One-bedroom house

This classic design is creative, trendy and cheap. The cabin comprises a concrete foundation, with the rest being thickly layered iron sheets. This traditional one-bedroom home is perfect if you are looking for small mabati house designs in Kenya.

5. Thick mabati house

Gauges are the units used to measure the thickness of a mabati. This cabin design uses thick iron sheets to create a compact, noiseless home. The house is constructed using gauge 26 or 28 mabati and has an elegant brown finish to match the rural setting.

6. Simple mabati house

If you are interested in low-cost mabati house designs in Kenya, this simple mabati house plan is ideal. The units comprise corrugated iron sheets and wood for structure. Though not a perfect permanent solution, such units are durable and can accommodate you considerably.

7. Mabati prefab house

The prefab house concept comprises building off-site and shipping the finished product. With this concept, you can opt for the design you want. The advantage of this system is that you'll not incur labour charges compared to a home built on-site.

8. Three bedrooms with a front porch

This three-bedroom unit comprises a concrete foundation, with the rest of the house completed using iron sheets and wood. The cabin has a front porch with stylish shading that is part of the roofing.

9. Large, simple mabati house

If you think building large equals deep pockets, you are in for a pleasant surprise. This large, simple iron sheet unit is cheap and affordable to complete. The design comprises a standard rectangular plan ideal for a large family.

10. Modern mabati house design

This mabati house combines modern design with traditional materials to create a beautiful and affordable home. The cabin features a housetop roof and has a small porch with a wooden shelter, providing a shaded outdoor space. There are two windows, one on the front and one on the side, allowing plenty of natural light to fill the interior.

Mabati house interior design

The key to a beautiful mabati house interior design is creativity and efficient use of space. Despite their economical construction, these houses can have beautiful and functional interior designs. Here are some features that you can include in your mabati house interior design:

  • Kitchen: You can equip the kitchen with a stove, dining table, cupboards, and a fridge.
  • Living Room: You can include a comfortable living room set with a coffee table.
  • Bedroom: You can fit a comfortable bed and necessary storage units.
  • Bathroom: You can fit a functional bathroom with all the necessary amenities.
