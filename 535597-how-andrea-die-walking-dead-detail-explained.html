Why did Andrea die so early in The Walking Dead?

The Walking Dead creator Robert Kirkman attributed Andrea's early death to a suggestion by former showrunner Glen Mazzara in the writers' room. Robert explained that her death made sense due to all the twists and turns that the Woodbury story had taken in season 3.

In an interview with The Hollywood Reporter in April 2013, Robert Kirkman said:

We thought having that tragic end to her story line — having [Andrea] work so hard to save these people of Woodbury and find a peaceful resolution to this conflict was really something worthy of the character. It’s also something that changes Rick’s (Andrew Lincoln) mindset and alters a lot of the characters and their modes of operation moving into the fourth season. It seemed like the right thing to do at the right time.

The Walking Dead creator also discussed Andrea's death and how it served as a motivator for the characters. He thought it was the appropriate decision for the show since it made the Governor a fearsome adversary in season 4 and drove Rick in the direction the writers wanted him to go.

How does Andrea die in the Walking Dead comics?

In The Walking Dead comics, Andrea Grimes's death came much later than the death of the character in the show. In issue 167, she is bitten on the neck by a walker, and Rick is forced to kill her so she wouldn't reanimate as a walker. Unlike in the television series, she passes in a heroic moment of self-sacrifice as she defends the newly regrouped saviours.
