Ahmednasir Abdullahi: 5 Multi-Million Properties Owned by Kenyan Lawyer

Publish date: 2024-06-23
  • Senior counsel Ahmednasir Abdullahi has practised law for over 20 years representing political bigwigs such as President Uhuru Kenyatta and DP William Ruto
  • During the 2013 presidential elections petition, he was reportedly paid KSh 40 million by the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) for legal services
  • The city lawyer nicknamed the Grand Mullah owns a multi-million mansion in the affluent Karen neighbourhood in Nairobi

City lawyer Ahmednasir Abdullahi lives an opulent life eating life with a big spoon.

The senior counsel does not shy away from displaying his flamboyance to all and sundry.

Having been a seasoned lawyer for over 20 years, Ahmednasir has accumulated wealth millions of shillings as he has represented bigwigs in the Kenyan political scene.

During the 2013 presidential elections petition, he was reportedly paid KSh 40 million by the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) for legal services.

What does Ahmednasir own?

1. Ahmednasir's mansion in Karen

In August 2021 while appearing on Churchill Show, the lawyer nicknamed the Grand Mullah showed Kenyans a tour of his multi-million mansion in Karen.

As reported by TUKO.co.ke, the revered 'learned friend' finally displayed how blessed he and his family are.

From the video shared on YouTube, one would think he was walking into a hotel by the first view of Ahmednasir's home, which is simply magnificent.

The backyard is breathtaking, with a nice turf where people get to relax just next to the incredible swimming pool.

Speaking to Churchill (Daniel Ndambuki), the lawyer disclosed that every part of his home was paid for by a client, and when he was short of money for construction, he would always save a sum from his next case.

"You know when you are short for money then a client comes he pays for money then you buy the doors or windows, this and that. I have very fond memories that it is my clients who pay for this," he said.

2. Grand Mullah's cars

From his parking lot, Ahmednasir owns some of the most attractive cars you will find but appears to enjoy rides on his Mercedes Benz G-Wagon more often.

The clip showed a nice brown Porsche next to what appeared to be another Porshe SUV, blue in colour.

One could also spot two Mercedes Benz G-Wagon rides, a blue Bentley SUV next to a green Land Rover Discovery 3.

In August 2020, the court directed the Kenya National Highway Authority (KeNHA) to pay Ahmednasir KSh 750,000 in compensation after stones from a road construction site along Nairobi- Namanga road damaged his KCQ 744Y Bentley Bentyaga's windscreen.

3. Ahmednasir's farm

During the same show, the Grand Mullah revealed he has invested his money in land.

The renowned lawyer said that besides his law career, he enjoys farming at his 200-acre farm in Isinya, Kajiado county as a hobby.

Ahmednasir disclosed he owns 250 camels.

4. Brioni suits

Ahmednasir previously said he loves Brioni suits, with one costing him a whopping KSh 1.2 million.

"I have good taste in clothes - suits, shirts, ties and shoes. All my jeans are, and most of my suits are by Brioni. I’m into labels. I shop when I travel, and my clothes remind me of where I have been," he said.

In 2018, a Kenyan woman confronted him over what she described as 'Chinese suit'.

While hitting back, the lawyer stated he dons Brioni tailor-made suits.

“I probably should not bother to tell this to a little girl who has never heard of a Brioni tailor-made suit. But the suit I wore last evening for Jeff Koinange’s show is tailor-made by Brioni’s master tailor at KSh 1.2 million,” he said.

5. Expensive watch

The senior counsel is among a few elite Kenyans who have been spotted wearing expensive watches.

A while back he told Business Daily he doesn't buy fake things since he is rich.

“I have never bought a fake in my life. Never! I went to Hong Kong once with a lawyer friend of mine and bought this watch I’m wearing for KSh 870,000. I live for today. I don’t save money, I don’t care what happens tomorrow. Only God has plans for tomorrow.

Francis Atwoli's wealth

TUKO.co.ke previously reported COTU boss Francis Atwoli accumulated most of his wealth in a career spanning over 50 years.

Born in Webuye in 1949, the combative labour leader has been serving as the secretary-general of COTU since his first election to the position in 2001.

Atwoli owns multi-million homes in Kajiad, the Coast and Kwhisero.
