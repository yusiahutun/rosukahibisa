How to fix the shift lock release button

The shift lock release button is an essential feature in any car. Without the button, you cannot move the gearshift between the park and the neutral position. Also, the button prevents accidental shifting of the gear lever while the car is in motion. Therefore, if it is not working, you must fix it immediately. Here are some steps you should follow:

Check for loose connections

First, check the shift lock release button for loose connections. Generally, loose connections in the shift lock release button wiring can cause it to malfunction. Also, loose wiring can cause an incomplete connection resulting in erratic or intermittent button operation.

If the connections are loose, tighten them up and see if that fixes the issue. Also, inspect the wiring and tighten any loose connections. Additionally, check for damage or corrosion in the wiring. And where necessary, replace any damaged wiring.

Check the fuse box

If you have determined that you do not have loose connections and everything is secure, the next step is to check the fuse box. Generally, the fuse box provides power to the shift lock release button, unlocking the shifter to allow you to move it from one gear to another.

Checking the fuse box will help determine if the fuse associated with the shift lock release button has blown. In that case, the shifter will be locked and not move. Therefore, you will have to replace the fuse.

Check the shift lock release cable

The shift lock release cable is connected to the shifter handle and transmission. Its work is to prevent a car from being shifted out of park without depressing the brake pedal. Once a driver depresses the brake pedal, it releases the cable. As a result, the driver can shift the vehicle from the park position.

Reset the shift lock release mechanism

Resetting the shift release mechanism achieves several goals. First, it will override the shift interlock solenoid and move the gear selector from the park to the neutral position. The step is crucial when the car is stuck in the park, and a driver needs to move it manually.

Secondly, resetting the shift lock release mechanism helps prevent damaging the transmission when the gear selector is stuck in the park position. Therefore, resetting ensures the driver does not put the transmission under more strain. To reset the mechanism:

  • Engage the emergency or parking brake
  • Turn the ignition key to the ON position.
  • Locate the shift lock override slot in the shifter.
  • Press and hold the shift lock release button for five seconds. Use a flat-head screwdriver to press and hold it.

What causes the gear shift to get stuck?

A gear shift can get stuck because of wear and tear in the gearbox. Wear and tear can happen over time due to the driving technique when shifting gears.
