Brian Chira's Grandma Recalls Calling Him after Landlord Demanded Rent Unaware He Was Dead

Publish date: 2024-04-04
  • Brian Chira's grandmother recalled calling him to speak about rent arrears, but his phone was off
  • The heartbroken grandmother said she tried calling Chira several times, but he was unreachable; little did she know he was no more
  • Chira became famous after giving an eyewitness account of a gruesome accident before and was among the most followed TikTokers in Kenya

Kenyans are still in disbelief following the death of TikToker Brian Chira.

How did Brian Chira die?

Chira was loved and hated in equal measure after becoming famous. He shared an eyewitness statement with a journalist in a video that went viral online, thrusting him into the limelight.

Chira witnessed a road accident in which eight people died and 16 were injured. The accident happened on date 16, and coincidentally, Chira died on the same date years later, leaving netizens in shock.

The TikToker tragically lost his life in a hit-and-run accident in Kiambu County. Details indicated that the content creator was crossing the road when he was struck by a speeding vehicle.

One of Chira's relatives talked of the TikToker's demise, noting that he was heartbroken.

Brian Chira's grandmother speaks

A section of TikTokers led by Baba Talisha visited Chira's home in Githunguri, where he lived with his grandmother.

They were welcomed by his grandmother, who raised him, revealing she was shocked by Chira's sudden demise.

"Shosho alikuwa anampigia simu leo (Saturday) because landlord alikuwa anasumbua kidogo. Alikuwa anampigia simu ajue vile watasaidiana kidogo lakini alikuwa mteja. Hata alikuwa amempikia chakula anangojea akuje kula lakini simu yake ilikuwa off. Until now, simu iko off (His grandmother called him today (Saturday) because the landlord wanted rent She was calling to find out how they could sort it, but he was unreachable. She had even cooked food for him and was waiting for him to come and eat, but his phone was off. Until now, the phone is still off)," Baba Talisha said.
