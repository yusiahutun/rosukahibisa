&quot;Femininity and feminism complement each other&quot;

Publish date: 2024-09-19

There has long been held a false belief by many that feminism and femininity are mutually exclusive, but Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie argues that the opposite is true, saying that the two actually "complement" each other.

In a new video for Dior, which you can watch exclusively above, the Nigerian writer discusses the narrow perception of feminism, adding that women "should be allowed to have a broad range".

"I think, for a long time, people felt that to be a feminist meant somehow to reject everything to do with femininity – that you have to in some ways kind of appropriate a man to be a feminist – but I don’t think so at all," she says. "Femininity and feminism actually do complement each other. Women should be allowed to have a broad range of what they can be, of who they can be, of how they can present themselves. I think femininity is one of those options and I love femininity."

Brigitte Lacombe

Adichie's musings echo those of Dior's creative director, Maria Grazia Chiuri, who has championed a message of femininity and sisterhood since taking over the helm of the fashion house in 2016 – seen perhaps most blatantly through her now-cult "we should all be feminists" T-shirt, which Adichie wears a version of in the video.

The new short film was created to mark the reinvention of one of Dior's most popular bag styles, the Lady Dior. To celebrate the launch of the new Lady 95.22 (so named to reflect the year 1995, the date of the icon's creation, and 2022, the year of this original reinterpretation), Adichie joins a host of other personalities from the worlds of art, cinema, sports, literature, fashion and music, who will star in similar videos throughout the coming year.

Brigitte Lacombe

"There's something almost timeless about the bag," says Adichie. "Its beauty is not rooted in any particular period. Timelessness means that it cuts across cultures, so it doesn’t matter where one is looking at a beautiful timeless thing from, one finds it."

The Half of a Yellow Sun author leaves us with one final piece of advice: "One of the mottos I like to live by is 'never admire quietly'. If I admire something about somebody I think it’s important to tell them. We humans are so fragile and it’s important to give people their flowers while they’re here. So never admire quietly."
