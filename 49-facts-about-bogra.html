Bogra is the oldest city in Bangladesh.

With its roots dating back to the 4th century BCE, Bogra boasts a rich historical heritage, making it a must-visit destination for history enthusiasts.

The city is home to the renowned Mahasthangarh archaeological site.

Mahasthangarh, also known as ‘The Citadel of the Mohasthan,’ is one of the most ancient archaeological sites in South Asia, stretching over 2,500 years of history.

Bogra is famous for its terracotta artwork.

The city is known for its exquisite terracotta sculptures, which can be found adorning many historic structures, adding a touch of intricate beauty.

FAQs

1. What are some must-visit archaeological sites in Bogra?
Some must-visit archaeological sites in Bogra include Mahasthangarh, Shalban Bihar, and Paharpur Buddhist Monastery.

2. What is the best time to visit Bogra?
The best time to visit Bogra is during the winter months, from November to February, when the weather is pleasant and suitable for outdoor activities.

3. How can I reach Bogra?
Bogra is well-connected by road and rail. You can either take a bus or train from Dhaka or hire a private car for a more comfortable journey.

4. What are some popular festivals in Bogra?
Some popular festivals in Bogra include the Shakrain Festival, which is celebrated with kite flying and fireworks, and the Pahela Baishakh, the Bengali New Year.

5. What are some local delicacies to try in Bogra?
Some local delicacies to try in Bogra include Bogra Bhapa Pitha, Shorshe Ilish, and Misti Polao.

6. Are there any accommodations available in Bogra?
Yes, Bogra offers a range of accommodations including hotels, resorts, and guesthouses to suit different budgets and preferences.

7. Is Bogra safe for tourists?
Bogra is generally a safe city for tourists. However, it is always advisable to take standard precautions and follow the local laws and customs while traveling.

8. What are some nearby attractions to visit from Bogra?
