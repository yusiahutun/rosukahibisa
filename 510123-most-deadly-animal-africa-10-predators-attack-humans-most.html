Most deadly animal in Africa: 10 predators that attack humans the most

Publish date: 2024-01-28

More than 30 million tourists visit Africa annually on safaris to observe wildlife in their natural habitats. The continent has vast open savanna plains and lush green woodlands, enabling nature to thrive. However, it is home to some of the world's most deadly animals in Africa.

Africa is a vast continent and home to a biodiversity of wildlife that makes embarking on a safari worth the while. Due to habitat encroachment, there is increasing human-wildlife conflict leading to fatalities.

10 most deadly animals in Africa

The continent is home to different species of animals. However, despite the pristine landscape and wildlife, the continent hosts some of the deadliest animals. Here are some of the most deadly animals in Africa, according to Travel Awaits:

1. Nile crocodile

What is the number 1 deadliest animal? Crocodiles are some of the deadliest animals that have survived various evolutionary changes dating over 200 million years ago. The reptiles are found in almost every major African water body or river and are ferocious predators.

The Nile crocodiles are dangerous freshwater predators responsible for over 1000 human deaths annually. They are stealthy creatures that wait in ambush for anything within their reach. Crocodile attacks happen indiscriminately in African villages that utilize rivers to quench their water needs, often leaving fatalities.

2. African lion

The African lion, classified as one of The Big Five on safaris, is one of the most dangerous animals in the world. Lions are apex predators that inhabit and roam the African jungle. They are responsible for over 200 human casualties annually.

They hunt by using their tough inclined canines to take down prey. Lions are also known to assert dominance and attack anything it perceives as a threat. Unfortunately, due to human encroachment into wildlife habitats, human-wildlife conflicts have often recurred with devasting effects.

3. Hippopotamus

Herbivorous hippopotamus could easily be mistaken as unthreatening creatures for their cute and bubbly demeanour. Despite primarily feeding on vegetation, hippos are territorial and attack unprovoked. Hippopotamus are directly responsible for an estimated 500 human deaths each year.

The average hippo weighs around 1,500 kilograms and can reach speeds up to 30 km/h. Hippopotamus are surprisingly fast for creatures of their body mass and agile in and out of water. They are hot temper aggressive and charge at random.

Most hippo-human attacks occur at night as they come out to feed. They are armed with razor-sharp, half-meter teeth that arch up to 150 degrees and are among the top 10 predators that attack humans.

4. African elephant

The African elephant is the largest land mammal on earth, standing over 10 feet tall. Elephants are recognizable worldwide for their massive trunks and huge tusks that make up their stature. They are important creatures in the ecosystem.

They clear and open new savanna lush lands clear for smaller animals to roam. An elephant is one of the most dangerous mammals in Africa and attacks humans unprovoked by charging at speeds and trampling anything in their path.

Territorial male elephants are incredibly aggressive during mating seasons when their testosterone levels are inflated. Despite annual reports of over 500 elephant-related deaths, they are still considered endangered and at risk of extinction.

5. Rhinoceros

Rhinos are not predators but are considered Africa's most dangerous land mammals for their aggressive demeanour. They have poor eyesight and easily flee threatened when anything encroaches too close to their territory.

Short-tempered rhinos are deadly, charge randomly unprovoked, and attain top speeds of 50km/h. They are responsible for over 200 human deaths annually, and habitat encroachment has increasingly led to human-wildlife conflict.

Rhinos are also poached for their horns and sold to Asian markets for alleged medicinal purposes. Extensive poaching has led to dwindling Rhino populations, with black rhinos listed as critically endangered and the white rhino extinct.

6. Puff adder

The puff adder is a deadly viper in Africa with potent venom that releases potent cytotoxic toxins. The viper is an intuitive attacker using camouflage and bright skin to entice prey before striking a fatal bite. It has earned a negative reputation and is commonly called the demon snake.

What animals cause the most deaths in Africa? This snake causes 32,000 human deaths annually, more than any other African snake recording over 90% of all snake bites. A puff adder bite sends through blistering pain followed by swelling and severe tissue haemorrhage if untreated with an antivenom within hours.

7. Black mamba

The black mamba is one of Africa's fastest reptiles, attaining top speeds of 20km/h. The black mamba has earned a fierce reputation due to its aggressive nature and reportedly around 3000 black mamba-related deaths annually.

The black mamba laces prey with its lethal venom, and only two drops of the liquid packs a lethal punch enough to kill an adult. Ferocious predators, black mambas inhabit rocky hills and open savannas of south and eastern Africa, growing up to 14 feet in length.

8. Leopard

The Leopard is part of the big cat family and inhabits the African savanna plainlands. Leopards are carnivores and are constantly on the hunt for prey from treetops. The predators lay, sleep, and spot unsuspecting prey and ambush.

They can lift twice as much as their body weight back to the tree tops to fend on it. Although human-leopard encounters are rare, they have occurred due to prolonged drought and leopard habitat encroachment.

As civilizations expand, humans encroach into more leopard territory for residential and agricultural land use. Human–leopard attacks are becoming frequent and responsible for over 30 deaths annually.

9. Hyena

The hyena is a scavenger native to the African sub-continent. The hyena is not a natural predator rather, the carnivore fends and scavenges on the carcasses of other animals maintaining the delicate wildlife ecosystem.

Unlike other scavengers, the hyena possesses powerful jaws that can crush, break down bones and tear apart flesh. In Africa, hyenas are responsible for some 300 annual human deaths as they encroach into human settlements to feed on livestock, and the ensuing conflict leaves fatalities as collateral.

10. Cape buffalo

The buffalo is among the big five animals that inhabit the semi-arid African savanna lowland. The Cape Buffalo is prone to launch unprovoked attacks and is responsible for over 200 human deaths.

Cape buffalos are grumpy by default and are unpredictable, earning the title of most deadly animal in South Africa. Café buffalos reach top speeds of 55km/h and are known to be aggressive, latching at humans, trampling or goring them with powerful horns.
