Serie A boss who headbutted rival striker is sacked with fans saying 'he thinks it's WWE'

Publish date: 2024-08-31

Serie A side Lecce have fired head coach Roberto D’Aversa after headbutting an opposition player following a defeat.

The 48-year-old lost his cool after Lecce's 1-0 defeat at home to Hellas Verona on Sunday (March 10) which left them 15th in the table and just one point above 18th-placed Frosinone.

Following the full-time whistle, D'Aversa confronted Verona striker Thomas Henry who was shown a straight red card in the 97th minute as well as the Lecce boss who was shown his marching orders by referee Daniele Chiffi.

After sharing a few words with Henry, D'Aversa lunged in and headbutted the Frenchman, causing him to clutch his head and fall to the ground. The Italian calmly walked away after the wild altercation but was sacked shortly after the incident.

Lecce released a statement on Sunday in which they said they "strongly condemned" D’Aversa’s actions which the club said were "contrary to the principles and values of sport".

The club released a second statement on Monday (March 11) which read: "After the events that occurred at the end of the Lecce-Verona match, US Lecce announces that it has relieved coach Roberto D’Aversa of his duties".

It added: "Thanks go to the coach and his staff for the work done". The headbutt seemed to be the breaking point in between the club and D'Aversa with Lecce having won just two league games since October. The German-born manager was appointed at the start of this season having signing a one-year deal with a further 12-month option if they avoided relegation.

Fans were left in shock with the Italian's actions with one X (formerly Twitter) user writing: "Bro thinks this is WWE" while another had a similar quip with: "A WWE manager in football".

A third user lamented Lecce's decision to sack D'Aversa as they commented: "Why would you sack a manager because of that? What about players who headbutt other players all the time? This is not a valid reason to sack a manager IMO." Meanwhile, a fourth explained exactly why his sacking was justified by replying: "Mate it's assault. What do you mean its not a valid reason".
