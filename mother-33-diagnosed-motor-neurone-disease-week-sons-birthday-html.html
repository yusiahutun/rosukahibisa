Mother, 33, was diagnosed with motor neurone disease a week after her son's first birthday

Publish date: 2024-09-26

An Adelaide mother, who lost four immediate family members to motor neurone disease, has been diagnosed with the illness just a week after her son's first birthday.

Lara Huddleston, 33, found out the news in September 2017 and because there is no cure, it means the new mum is terminal. She is currently in palliative care.

'They think I'm deteriorating quite fast because I don't get the chance to rest,' she told FEMAIL .

'They're tweaking my medication at the moment, I'm not sleeping because I'm highly anxious because I don't want to die.'

Lara Huddleston, 33, (right) was diagnosed with motor neurone disease, MND, only a week after her son Thomas's first birthday and moved her wedding with Adam Huddleston (left) forward so she could walk down the aisle

Lara isn't getting any rest because her husband is working 14 hours a day to support them, meaning she is at home looking after their 18-month-old son by herself.

To combat this, the hospice she is currently visiting is trying to set up a system where she can stay for a fortnight to rest every six to eight weeks.

Due to their family history, Lara and her sister knew they had a 50/50 chance of the disease.

Their father died from MND when she was 17, just a few years after he was diagnosed.

'He lasted three years. At the same time my dad was diagnosed my auntie was diagnosed with ALS, and she passed away six months after my dad did,' she told FEMAIL.

'So did my grandmother and my great grandmother and there are more in the family tree.

'I wasn't tested. I wanted to live my life normally and I had a beautiful baby boy but it's always been at the back of my head,' Lara added.

Lara found out the news in September 2017 and at present there is no cure, meaning she is terminal and is currently in palliative care

The reason that Lara isn't getting any rest is because while her husband is working 14 hours a day to support them, she is at home looking after their 18 month old son

WHAT IS MOTOR NEURONE DISEASE 

Motor neurone disease (MND) is a neurological disease which affects the nerves that control the muscles that enable us to move, breathe, speak and swallow.

When the nerve cells die, the muscles weaken and waste away. Lara said that this leaves the person a prisoner inside their own body.

MND is very broad as it's the name given to a group of diseases. They are:

Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS)

Progressive bulbar palsy (PBP)

Progressive muscular atrophy (PMA)

Primary lateral sclerosis (PLS)

'When I fell pregnant with Thomas we were really happy. He wasn't exactly planned and I was on the pill at the time, it was a miracle really,' Lara continued.

'I do believe having Thomas brought on the disease and activated the gene, I can't prove it but I had a traumatic child birth and developed symptoms after he was born.'

Lara explained that she put the symptoms down to being a new mum, but as time went on it was evident things were getting worse.

Soon she was tripping over and finding it difficult to support herself.

'I've always been a busy person, I was a registered nurse and I was very fit so if I fell it was easy for me to get up off the floor,' she explained. 

Due to their family history, Lara and her sister knew they had a 50/50 chance of the disease

'I wasn't tested. I wanted to live my life normally and I had a beautiful baby boy but it's always been at the back of my head,' Lara said 

'Eventually I had to rely on furniture to get up and one day my mum, who's 73, said jokingly,  "you get up like me",' Lara continued.

'I went quiet and I thought, "I do and that's not normal, I don't normally do that so there's something wrong here".'

When Lara was experiencing all of these falls she went to the doctor, who referred her to a neurologist. 

Lara believes that going through pregnancy and childbirth helped to bring on the disease faster

'When I fell pregnant with Thomas we were really happy. He wasn't exactly planned and I was on the pill at the time, it was a miracle really,' Lara said

It wasn't long after Lara (right) gave birth to Thomas (left) that she started experiencing symptoms of the disease, but put it down to being a new mum

One day when she suffered a particularly bad fall and bruised herself quite badly, her doctor made sure she got into the neurologist straight away.   

He examined Lara and said it could be one of three things - MS, a tumour on the spine or motor neurone disease.

When the MRI she had didn't show up MS or a tumour, she knew it was motor neurone disease.

Lara's dad passed away from MND when she was 17, after only being diagnosed at 14

Lara was diagnosed with ALS, a type of motor neurone disease called amyotrophic lateral sclerosis.

'It affects the nerves, the nerves in the brain and the spinal chord. It kills the nerve cells and it weakens muscles, so when the nerve cells die, the muscles die as well,' she said.

This meant she eventually found it hard to walk because it started in her legs.  

'When I got the diagnosis I just walked out of the office and screamed. Me and my fiance at the time sat on a bench and I didn't really say anything,' Lara explained.

'I just said "what the hell are we going to do". I know it's a fast progressing disease, the longest people have lived in my family is three years.

'I said "how can this possibly be happening to us, our lives are finally perfect, we have this beautiful baby boy and we have this horrible diagnosis".'

It was when Lara was experiencing a variety of falls that she was fast-tracked to a neurologist

Adam, who is now her husband, has proved to be her rock through the experience. 

'He was very quiet but he said "we will keep fighting and get through this". Since then I've just been trying to fight and educate people on the disease,' she shared.

'At the moment there isn't enough awareness about the disease and when there isn't enough awareness there often isn't enough money for research.' 

As her disease progresses, Lara finds it hard to do everyday tasks and even having a shower and dressing leaves her feeling like she has run a marathon. 

Despite this, Lara has tried to live each day to the best of her ability, making the most of the potentially limited time she may have.

Lara and Adam even got married so they could share their love with the world during a trying time and started a GoFundMe page.  

'The money donated will go towards expenses for leaving valuable memories behind for my family members, medical costs and equipment and a van for the electric wheelchair,' it reads.
