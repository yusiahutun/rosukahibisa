Inside the life of football great Frank Gifford

Publish date: 2024-08-12

Kennedy matriarch Ethel Skakel Kennedy was devastated by football great Frank Gifford's sudden death because the two had a very special 'Camelot' relationship – they seriously dated after her husband, Sen. Robert Kennedy, was assassinated in 1968. 

She later played Cupid, introducing one of her seven sons, Michael, to his future wife, Gifford's daughter, Vicki, according to bestselling Kennedy biographer Jerry Oppenheimer.

Gifford had become a frequent dinner guest of widow Ethel at hickory Hill, her fabled McLean, Virginia, estate in the mid-to-late 1970s. At the time, according to Oppenheimer's book, 'The Other Mrs. Kennedy: An American Drama of Power, Privilege, and Politics,' Gifford was in the process of getting a divorce from one of his several wives, Maxine, who had multiple sclerosis.

Ethel's friends revealed, 'If Frank wasn't married, she'd grab him in a minute.'

More than friends: Ethel Kennedy 'was crazy about Frank – big-time, teenager crazy,' her secretary at the time, Noelle Fell, told author Jerry Oppenheimer. 'When she knew he was coming for dinner, she'd giggle, she couldn't stand the excitement'

In an exclusive interview with Daily Mail Online, Oppenheimer noted that Ethel's secretary at the time, Noelle Fell, told him, 'Mrs. Kennedy was crazy about Frank – big-time, teenager crazy. When she knew he was coming for dinner, she'd giggle, she couldn't stand the excitement. 

'All the members of her staff would say, 'Frank Gifford's coming. Ethel will be a in a good mood now.

'Mrs. Kennedy would look the best she could for him. In the years I worked for her, I would have said he would be her pick for her next husband, even though I believe it was all one-sided.'

Oppenheimer reported that one of Ethel's college friends had attended a dinner party at Hickory Hill and that Ethel had 'set up a table for just her and Frank. It was very private and she never gave it a thought how it would look to everyone else who was present'.

And by the late 1970s, according to Ethel's biographer, the famed Kennedy widow, mother of eleven, and the NFL football hero and TV sports celebrity, made their private relationship more public, appearing together, dining and dancing at chic Georgetown clubs, igniting gossip column speculation that they might marry.

The former secretary Fell recalled that Mrs. Kennedy, now 87, thrived on compliments from the charming and handsome Gifford. 'She liked to hear how pretty she looked, what a good athlete she was. Mr. Gifford knew what she expected. She just loved the attention.'

Broken trust: Gifford's idolized view of his son-in-law all changed in the late-1990s when it was revealed that Kennedy, father of three, had been having a five year affair with his family's pretty teenage babysitter, which made headlines around the world.

But, according to the author, Ethel was never going to give up the Kennedy name. To friends, she was considered, 'More Kennedy than Kennedy, and idolized the clan, and especially her mother-in-law, Rose Kennedy, with whom she competed to have more children than Rose's nine,' he told Daily Mail Online.

But since Ethel was never going to give up her position in America's so-called royal family, Frank Gifford went on to marry the pretty and perky TV star Kathie Lee.

It was Ethel Kennedy who first introduced Gifford's daughter, Vicki, to Ethel's fourth-born son, Michael, according to Oppenheimer, and Gifford later claimed in a memoir, 'It was Ethel who brought my daughter, Vicki, into the Kennedy orbit.'

'Vicki was just sixteen and had been staying at Hickory Hill, and Michael was at boarding school, and Ethel played Cupid and brought them together. She even arranged for Vicki to have an internship in Ted Kennedy's senate office in Washington the summer that Vicki was staying at Ethel's estate,' the author said.

On March 14, 1981, Vicki Gifford became Mrs. Michael Kennedy in a big, fancy wedding at the Roman Catholic Park Avenue Church of St. Ignatius Loyola, in New York City, and a glitzy reception was held on the rooftop of the St. Regis Hotel, with dozens of famous guests.

In his memoir, 'The Whole Ten Yards,' Gifford wrote that his daughter had 'made a brilliant choice' by marrying Ethel's son, Michael. 

'Besides bearing a spooky resemblance to his father, Michael has inherited Bobby's intelligence and charm and concern for others…and like Bobby, Michael's a good daddy.'

The hotel tryst: Frank and Suzen met up at the Regency hotel in New York and Globe magazine had the proof- the rumpled bed, the unfinished bottle of Franks favorite wine and pictures of Frank leaving the hotel mopment s before Suzen. They also had pictures of them together kissing

But Gifford's idolized view of his son-in-law all changed in the late 1990s when it was revealed that Kennedy, father of three, had been having a five year affair with his family's pretty teenage babysitter, which made headlines around the world.

Kennedy was forced to go into rehab for alcohol addiction, and was considered to be a sex addict. 

Press reports at the time stated that his wife had caught him bed with the girl, and private school classmates were quoted as stating that she talked about her affair to them but they thought she was making it up until the news broke in the Boston Globe and other news media. Vicki Gifford Kennedy left her husband after sixteen years.

Kennedy came under investigation by the district attorney in Norwalk County, Massachusetts. But the babysitter, who had turned eighteen, decided not to be a witness against the 38-year-old Kennedy who faced possible statutory rape charges for having sex with someone under the age of sixteen, and the probe was dropped.

In his memoir, Vicki's father, said, 'If there's one myth about the Kennedys that ain't no myth, it's their incredible athletic competitiveness. Playing touch football with them at Hickory Hill or Hyannis Port was insanity.'

Johnson, who was from Florida, called Frank to let him know she was in New York. He suggested they meet up at her hotel

That touch football insanity happened once again on the slopes of Aspen, Colorado, on New Year's Eve 1998, when Michael Kennedy, playing touch football on skis with Kennedy family members and friends, caught a pass over his shoulder, and slammed headfirst into a tree. He died at a local hospital of massive head injuries. He was 39 years old. 

Like his adulterous late son-in-law, Frank Gifford also became entangled in a sex scandal. As author Oppenheimer told Daily Mail Online, 'Both father-in-law and son-in-law – Frank Gifford and Michael Kennedy – had a dark side, and were womanizers and philanderers.'

In 1992, Kathie Lee Gifford also wrote a memoir, 'I Can't Believe I Said That,' in which she asserted, 'Frank is my husband, confidante, lover, therapist, parenting partner, and best friend…Cheating is out of the question. Sure, Frank sees sexy flight attendants and businesswomen when he flies around the country. But the only come-on line he gets anymore is "C'mon, Frank, show me a picture of Cody",' one of their children about whom she often boasted on TV's popular Live with Regis & Kathie Lee.

But Kathie Lee's bliss about her seemingly faithful husband was punctured in 1997 when the supermarket tabloid Globe ran a front-page story exposing – with photos and a transcript – Gifford's sexual tryst with a sexy, married, 46-year-old TWA flight attendant named Suzen Johnson, who alleged that she and Gifford had been seeing each other for several years.

Johnson, who was from Florida, called Frank to let him know she was in New York. He suggested they meet at her hotel, the posh Regency. They drank his favorite wine and slipped into the bedroom of the suite where they had passionate sex. When Kathie Lee found out she was devastated. 

People magazine called it 'Kathie Lee's Crises.'
