Gloom is a Grass/Poison type Pokémon.

Gloom is known for its unique dual typing, which makes it resistant to many common types of attacks.

Gloom evolves from Oddish when exposed to a Leaf Stone.

When an Oddish is exposed to a Leaf Stone, it undergoes a dramatic transformation and evolves into Gloom.

Gloom’s appearance is characterized by its droopy plant-like body.

Gloom has large petals on its head that resemble a plant’s leaves. Its body is often droopy, giving it a somewhat sad expression.

Gloom has a powerful ability called “Stench.”

Stench is an ability unique to Gloom, which has a chance to cause the opponent’s Pokémon to flinch when using a move in battle.

Gloom emits a foul odor from the flowers on its head.

The unpleasant smell that Gloom emits from its flowers can be used as a defense mechanism to ward off predators.

Gloom is known for its unique move, “Acid Spray.”

Acid Spray is a Poison-type move that Gloom can learn, which deals damage to the opponent and lowers their Special Defense.

Gloom is often found in damp and shady areas, such as forests.

Gloom prefers to live in moist environments, where it can thrive among plants and vegetation.

Gloom’s evolution line is based on the stages of a plant’s life cycle.

Oddish represents the seed, Gloom represents the budding plant, and Vileplume represents the fully grown flower.

Gloom has the ability to release a sweet scent when it feels happy.

Despite its occasional unpleasant odor, Gloom can emit a delightful fragrance when it’s in a joyful mood.

Gloom’s diet mainly consists of plants and herbs.

Gloom feeds on a variety of vegetation, utilizing photosynthesis to convert sunlight into energy.

Gloom is a popular Pokémon among herbalists and botanists.

Gloom has a hidden ability called “Gluttony.”

Gluttony allows Gloom to consume held berries when its HP drops below 50% instead of waiting until it’s at a critical health point.

Gloom’s signature move is “Petal Blizzard.”

Petal Blizzard is a powerful Grass-type move that Gloom can unleash in battles, causing widespread damage to opponents.

Gloom’s unique physiology allows it to absorb nutrients from the soil it walks on.

Gloom’s flowers can change color based on its health and mood.

The color of Gloom’s flowers can vary from vibrant and healthy shades to dull and wilted tones, reflecting its overall condition.

FAQs

1. How does Gloom evolve?

Gloom evolves from Oddish starting at level 21. It further evolves into Vileplume when exposed to a Leaf Stone or into Bellossom when exposed to a Sun Stone.

2. Can Gloom learn any unique moves?

Yes, Gloom can learn several unique moves, including Sludge Bomb, Petal Dance, Solar Beam, and Acid Spray.

3. What are Gloom’s strengths and weaknesses?

Gloom’s grass-poison type gives it an advantage over water, ground, and rock type Pokémon. However, it is weak against fire, flying, ice, and psychic type Pokémon.

4. Is Gloom rare in the wild?

Gloom is relatively common in areas with a high concentration of grass and plant life. It can be found in forests, gardens, and meadows.

5. Can Gloom be shiny?

Yes, Gloom has a shiny variant that has a different color palette. Shiny Gloom has a darker purple body and green leaves instead of red.
