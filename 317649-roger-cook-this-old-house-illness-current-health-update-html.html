Roger Cook's biography

How old is Roger Cook, and where does he hail from? The renowned former television star was born in 1955 in Massachusetts, the United States of America. He spent a significant portion of his childhood in Massachusetts, where his interest in landscaping began.

Cook then attended the University of Maine, from where he graduated with a Bachelor of Science in Wildlife Management and Conservation law in 1977.

Immediately after completing his education, Roger joined the Massachusetts Arborist Association and has been an active member ever since. Later, he started his business, K&R Tree and Landscape Company, in 1982 with his wife Kathleen (who died in 2010).

In 1982, Roger was invited to appear in the reality TV show This Old House after contributing to several of the home renovation show's episodes. However, it wasn't until 1988 that he was made part of the primary cast, and his popularity increased.

What happened to Roger Cook on This Old House?

If you are an avid fan, you might have noticed Roger Cook from This Old House making fewer appearances on the show and then eventually leaving the show entirely. In June 2018, the famous landscaper issued a health statement stating he was battling several medical conditions.

What happened to Roger Cook from This Old House? In an open letter, the landscaper stated that he had enjoyed his job for the many years he was on the show and is still proud to be part of the cast. He also acknowledged the friends and family he made while on the show before declaring that he would reduce his TV roles due to his health issues.

Besides This Old House, Roger was also part of popular spin-offs called Inside This Old House and Ask This Old House. In the letter, the experienced contractor said that he intends to use the time off from work to tend to his health while spending quality time with his family.

Why is Roger Cook limping?

Fans of This Old House also probably noticed that Cook had begun walking with a limp. This led to rumours that Cook had Parkinson's disease. Parkinson's is a neurological disease caused by a decreased production of dopamine, a neurotransmitter released by neurons to other nerve cells.

This progressive disease can cause impaired balance, slow movement, or even resting tremors. Some of these symptoms can also be witnessed after a stroke or in cancer patients.
